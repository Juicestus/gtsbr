407 What is encryption?

Encryption is a defense against people who are trying to steal data. It works by making the data meaningless so that the data is useless if it is stolen. It only holds meaning to those with the means to decrypt it. Process of using an algorithm to convert.

407 What is cryptography?

The science of secret writing. Encoding plaintext prior to sending and decrypted at the end.

407 Why would you use secret writing?

To keep a messages contents secret in case it is stolen.

408 What are the formats of text?

Plaintext is the undecrypted message.

Ciphertext is the encrypted message.

409 What is decryption?

The reverse of encryption, bringing meaning to an encrypted message.

409 What is a symmetric encryption algorithm?

Requires the use of a secret key known to both the sender and receiver.

409 What the main drawback of symmetric encryption algorithm?

The main drawback is that it is difficult to send the key securely and secretly.

409 What is an asymmetric encryption algorithm?

Uses a public and private key that are related but not similar. One encrypts and decrypts for the other.

409 What are the benefits and drawbacks of asymmetric encryption algorithm?

This solves the problem of transferring a single key, but introduces a problem about the relationship between the keys.

409 What else is asymmetric encryption called?

Public key encryption.

409 What is a Ceasar cipher?

Also called a shift cipher, involves shifting each character in the message to another character a fixed distance father along in the alphabet.

410 What is the Ceasar cipher an example of?

A stream cipher and a substitution cipher.

410 What is a stream cipher?

A cipher that can encode one digit at a time in a data stream.

410 What is a substitution cipher?

A single letter of plaintext corresponds to a single letter of ciphertext.

410 What is a block cipher?

A digit of plaintext coverts into a block of ciphertext. This destroys the structure of the plaintext, making it harder to decrypt.

410 What is this block called?

A matrix.

413 What is DES?

Data Encryption Standard. Developed by IBM in thee 1970’s and adopted by the National Bureau of Standards (Now the NIST) and is an ISO standard. 64 bit block, 56 bit key. Created in response to encryption techniques like the ceaser cipher and block cipher being too simplistic. Used simple operations repeatedly.

417 What is triple DES?

Repeating and combining DES 3 times for extra security.

414 What is steganography?

Hiding data in plain sight, like within an image on a webpage.

417 What is AES?

The advanced encryption system, proposed in 1997 to combat increases in computing power. Keys can be 128, 192, or even 256.

417 What is RSA?

The most common public key encryption method. Based on number theory and prime numbers. Developed by MIT in 1977.

420 What is SSL?

Secure Socket Layer. The proprietary web transition security layer developed by Netscape in the mid 90s. (Netscape was an early web browser)

420 What is TLS?

Transport Layer Security. Almost identical to SSL but not proprietary. Fits between the Transport Layer with TCP and the Application layer with HTTP.

421 What is a handshake?

The setup for exchange of information.

422 What is an embedded computer?

A computer system, chip, or other computational device embedded within a another system like a car, oven, or factory machine.
